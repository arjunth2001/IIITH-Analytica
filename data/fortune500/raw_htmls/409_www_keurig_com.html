KDP PRIVACY POLICY

The KDP Privacy Policy applies to information that Keurig Dr Pepper Inc and its subsidiaries (collectively, “KDP” or “we” ,“us” , or “our”) collect from or about you, or that you provide, when you use our websites (each a “Site”) and mobile applications (each an “App”), (collectively, the “Services”). We appreciate that you trust us with your information and we intend to honor that trust.

This Privacy Policy is incorporated into and made a part of the KDP Terms of Use. If you have not done so already, please also review the KDP Terms of Use. By using the Services, you agree to the collection, use, and disclosure of your information as described in this Privacy Policy, and agree to the KDP Terms of Use, which are incorporated by reference.

GENERAL INFORMATION

This Privacy Policy applies to the Services to which it is linked. If a particular Service has its own privacy policy, then that policy – not this Privacy Policy – applies. This Privacy Policy does not apply to information that is collected by any third-party websites or services that you may access through the Services.

 

There are several ways we may obtain information about you, including through (A) information you provide to us directly; (B) information that we automatically collect; and (C) information we receive from third parties. Some of the information directly or indirectly identifies you as an individual. When information identifies or is capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with you or your household, we refer to it as “Personal Information.” Examples include identifiers (such as your name, email, address, phone number), Location Data (defined below), commercial or purchase-related information (such as your payment information or photos you share from your devices), Internet or other electronic network activity, and inferences drawn from your Personal Information to help us better serve you. Throughout this Privacy Policy references to information includes Personal Information. When information does not identify or is not capable of being associated with, or could not reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with you or your household, such as de-identified or aggregated information, we refer to it as “Other Information.”
