Privacy Policy

Marathon Petroleum Corporation, its affiliates and subsidiaries recognize and respect the privacy of our customers, employees, applicants and contractors, as well as other visitors to this website.  This privacy policy explains what personal data we collect from visitors to our websites and from our customers, employees, applicants and contractors.

(1) Collection of Personal Data from Site Visitors. As you navigate within our website, we may automatically collect information in aggregate form to track data such as the total number of visits to our website, the number of visitors to each page of our website, and the domain names of our website visitors’ internet service providers. We use this information, which remains in aggregate form, to understand how our visitors use our website and to help improve the website and visitor experience. That information may include:

  • Your IP address
  • Your device identifier
  • Your internet-service provider’s name
  • Browser type and language
  •  Operating system
  • Session identification number
  • Search terms, search results
  •  Online browsing data or “clickstream data” (e.g., date and time of access to the website, referring website address, pages viewed, features used, links clicked, and other actions you take in connection with the website)

We or other third parties may place internet cookies on your hard drive. When you revisit our website, we may recognize you by the internet cookie and customize your experience accordingly. These internet cookies are stored on your hard drive and are not stored, in any form, by us. You may decline the internet cookie by using the appropriate feature of your web client software, if available. You can find more information about cookies and how they work at https://www.allaboutcookies.org/.

We use cookies only in connection with your interactions with our website over time, and do not use them to track your online activities across third-party websites and other online services. If you wish to apply for a job with us through our careers website, you will be asked to provide information about yourself and your employment history, such as your name, address, e-mail address, and employment and educational history. You may also voluntarily opt in to receive emails notifying you of available jobs that match your employment and educational history and career interests. You may opt out of receiving these emails at any time. We will use the information you submit to determine whether you have the qualifications for a position for which you apply as well as for future job openings. We will share the information only with those who have a need to know the information for the purposes for which it was submitted. See also Section (6) below.

If you do not reside in the United States and provide personal information while visiting our website, including providing personal information on our careers section of the website in order to apply for a position outside of the United States, such as Canada, your personal information will be transferred, processed, collected, used, accessed and/or stored in the United States. Do not provide your personal information to us if you do not want this information to be transferred or processed outside of your country, or if the laws in your country restrict such transfers.

(2) Collection of Other Personal Data. If you send electronic mail messages to us, contact us through the website, or call us on the phone, we may retain the personal data you provide to us, including your name, email address, physical address and telephone number.

(3) Use of Personal Data Collected. We will use personal data collected from our website for statistical analysis of users’ behavior, product development, content improvement, and to customize content and layout of our website. We may also use your IP address to help diagnose problems with our servers, and to administer our website.  If you have contacted us via electronic mail, through the website or by calling us on the phone, we will may use your personal data in responding to your inquiry, improving our services to you and other customers, and tracking the inquiries we receive.

(4) Disclosure of Personal Data to Third Parties. We do not sell any of the personal data we collect to third parties. We do not post or otherwise provide individual personal information collected through our website to third parties. We will notify you before posting personal data collected from our website on the website, or sharing or otherwise disclosing individual personal data to third parties.

We may disclose non-identifying, aggregated information collected through our website to third parties for a variety of purposes, but only in a way that would not allow you or any other person to be identified.

(5) Opt-Out Right. You may opt out of having your personal data retained by us or used by us for secondary purposes by contacting us via postal mail or telephone. You may contact us at Marathon Petroleum Corporation, Customer Relations, 539 South Main Street, Findlay, OH 45840 or 1-866-462-7284.

(6) Employee, Applicant and Contractor Data. We collect and maintain personal information of those individuals who seek to be, are, or were employed by us, and of those individuals who are, or will, be temporary or long-term contractors of ours. This personal information includes, but is not limited to your:

  • first and last name, email address, telephone number, physical address, educational history, date of birth;
  • beneficiary and emergency contact information;
  • social security number, check deposit information;
  • references, photographs and video, letters of offer and acceptance of employment;
  • payroll information, wage and benefit information;
  • forms relating to the application for, or changes to, employee health and welfare benefits, including but not limited to medical and dental care, and short and long term disability and employment history; and
  • your children’s and partner/spouse’s (if any) personal information.

We collect and maintain the personal information for the following purposes:

  • determining eligibility for initial employment, including the verification of references and qualifications;
  • administering pay and benefits, including to employee’s children and partner/spouse (if any);
  • processing employee work-related claims (e.g. worker compensation and insurance claims);
  • establishing training and/or development requirements;
  • conducting performance reviews and determining performance requirements;
  • administration;
  • travel and expenses reimbursement;
  • health and safety and occupational health;
  • audit and security compliance;
  • assessing qualifications for a particular job or task;
  • gathering evidence for disciplinary action, or termination;
  • establishing a contact point in the event of an emergency (such as next of kin);
  • complying with applicable labor or employment rules, regulations, and statutes;
  • compiling directories;
  • ensuring the security of company information; and
  • or such other purposes as are reasonably required by us.


(7) Collection of Personal Data from Children. We will not knowingly collect or post information from children under the age of 18 without a parent or guardian’s consent. The parent or guardian has the right, upon request, to view the information provided by the child or to require that it be deleted. We do not provide any personally identifying information collected from children, regardless of its source, to any third party for any purpose whatsoever. We will not use information collected from children for any marketing or promotional purposes whatsoever, either inside or outside our company.

(8) Right to Contact User. We reserve the right to contact site visitors regarding changes to this privacy policy or any other policies or agreements relevant to such visitors. 

(9) Right to Change Privacy Policy. We reserve the right to change this policy at any time by posting a notice on our website of the existence and location of the new or revised privacy policy. Such posting shall be the sole notice required to make the new or revised policy effective. Please check this policy on a regular basis.

(10) Security. Security of your personal information is important and we honor your choices for its intended use. Once received, we work to protect your data from loss, misuse, unauthorized access or disclosure, alteration, or destruction. However, if we receive a subpoena or other enforceable order to disclose information, we will need to comply. In addition, if we are sold, assigned, merged, etc., the successor company may receive and use your data subject to its own privacy policy.

(11) Other Sites. This privacy policy applies only to our websites. While our websites may contain links to other third-party sites, we are not responsible for the practices or contents of such third-party sites.

(12) Enforcement. If for some reason you believe we have not adhered to this policy, please notify us through the Contact Us Section and we will do our best to determine and correct the problem promptly. Be certain the words “Privacy Policy” are in the subject line.

(13) California Residents.  If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Ask us to send you the following information:

      o   The categories of personal information we have collected about you.

      o   The categories of sources from which we collected the personal information.

      o   Our business or commercial purpose for collecting personal information.

      o   The categories of third parties with whom we share personal information.

      o   The specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you.

  • Ask us to delete the personal information we have collected from you.

To exercise the rights outlined above with respect to your personal information, you may submit a request at Marathon Petroleum Corporation, Customer Relations, 539 South Main Street, Findlay, OH 45840 or by calling 1-866-462-7284. For any written request please include “CCPA” in your notice.  We do not discriminate against California consumers who exercise their rights under the CCPA.
