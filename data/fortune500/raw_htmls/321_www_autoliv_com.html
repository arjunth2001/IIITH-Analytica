Privacy policy

This Privacy Policy describes how we handle data for our services on this Autoliv Inc. website (“autoliv.com”), which kind of data we collect, store, and process during your use of the publicly accessible parts of the website, for which purpose we collect, store, and process the data, and how we use the data.

Collection and processing of personal data

You can generally browse the Website without telling us who you are or revealing any other personal information about yourself. For system-related and statistical purposes, including providing a better service in the future, we will however automatically record every access to the website and every download of a file stored on the website. The log server protocol will include the name of the accessed file, time and date of the access, the volume of data transmitted, confirmation of successful download, web browser, operating system, the referring domain, and the IP address of the enquiring computer. This also includes the purpose of maintaining a sustainable and reliable level of IT-security in your and Autoliv’s interest, as we detect possible intrusion attempts and have measures in place that ensure your and Autoliv’s security.

In addition, we record the personal data (such as name, address and e-mail address) such that you enter in forms on the website and process it for sending requested material and/or information about us as well as for the respective purposes indicated in connection with the form on the website. The logged data and personal data will not be merged with data from other sources and will not be passed on to third parties outside the Autoliv Group (“Third Parties”), and the IP address will only be stored as long as necessary and will not be passed on to Third Parties.

Use of cookies

A „cookie“ is a small string of information that the website saves temporarily on your computer when you visit the website. Cookies help us in creating more comfortable and efficient offers on the website, including routing user traffic correctly and facilitating excel workbook navigation. Cookies pose no risk to your computer, as they are simply text files and not executable programs. We use various cookies that enable us to provide you the services and information that you are looking for. You can disable cookies in your internet browser options at any time and delete existing cookies. You will find further details in the online help of your browser. However, not all functionalities of the website are accessible without cookies. Your browser may already be set to display a warning whenever it receives a cookie. This warning can be very disruptive. We therefore recommend that you set your browser to always accept cookies from www.autoliv.com. You can change this setting for individual websites. You can find more information on the use of cookies and on how to deactivate cookies at www.meine-cookies.org or www.youronlinechoices.com

Some of the cookies we use are required to provide our website. Other cookies are used to better understand our visitors, to improve our website and services and to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. We introduce our use of cookies in the following sections.

We use so-called “session cookies” that will be stored only for the duration of your use of the website until you log off. They are part of our ADAM system used to maintain a persistent connection to the website. Load balancer cookies are implemented to keep track on what balanced node you are on, and we also use cookies to provide you with excel workbook navigation. In addition to this, we also use other categories of cookies: Essential Cookies, Functionality Cookies, Performance Cookies and Targeting and Advertising Cookies.This categorization mainly corresponds to that of the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC), UK Cookie guide published in April 2012, which may be accessed at the link below and which contains a description of these cookies).www.international-chamber.co.uk/components/com_wordpress/wp/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/icc_uk_cookie_guide.pdf
